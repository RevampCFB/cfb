Bowl Games

Inferior, playing superior, in bowl games

We often see inferior teams on paper and in popularity matched up against a much superior team in bowl games. Not usually does it result in the inferior beating the superior, but the opposite usually occurs.

The matchup of these two teams occurs when one runs the table in a less competitive conference while the other plays against stiffer competition throughout the season. Opt-outs and the draining enthusiasm for the opponent at hand may lead the inferior team to emerge victorious.

In 2023, Oregon was just shy of the College Football Playoffs, and because Liberty ran the table in their conference, the two teams would meet in the Vrbo Fiesta Bowl. The game resulted in the slaughter of Liberty.

Opt Outs

Players opting out of bowl play

Fans and colleges invest in student-athletes who are now paid professionals. Many athletes opt out of bowl games, which can tank a team. It's not so much "Who is the better team?" but "Which team has fewer opt-outs?"

You don't see opt-outs in college basketball when March Madness sweeps across the country. You also don't see opt-outs in college football when you make the playoffs. Why? Because those athletes have a shot at the championship.

With college football moving to a 12-team playoff system in 2024, the number of opt-outs will decrease, but only for those lucky 12 teams.

Financial

Ran by networks, teams earn more than they should

Under the current system, college teams compete within conferences, some outperforming others. However, this system allows specific teams to reap the benefits of others' success without contributing significantly to the conference's value, resulting in a glaringly unfair distribution of financial resources.

For example, in college football, money from the media runs conferences. If you're not in the big two, B1G and SEC, you could be left out with slim chances of ever benefiting from being in one of those conferences. Some schools carry the conference, and others do not. Yet, they might benefit from those that do.

According to Adam Rittenberg of ESPN, in 2022, the B1G landed a media rights deal with CBS, NBC, and FOX worth $7-8 billion. This would provide each member $62.5 million+ annually. Do all members help carry the conference? Do some schools deserve more than others?

The SEC inked a deal with ESPN, which will start in 2024. The contract is worth $3 Billion, about $50 million annually. Again, the question could be asked: does everyone in the SEC contribute the same value and deserve the same pay?

The Big 12 has a deal with ESPN, and FOX which will provide $31.67 million annually to each member.

Those held within the ACC's chains have something to complain about—so much so that, at this time, both Florida State and Clemson have filed lawsuits against the ACC for their media rights deal. These two teams are the first dominos to fall. Teams want to leave the conference, and the ACC seems doomed to join the same fate as the Pac-12.

Left Out

Teams left in the dark in conference realignment

When conference realignment happens, it has the potential for teams to be left without a home. Recently, the Pac-12 fell to pieces, and it looked like Washington St, Oregon St, Cal, and Stanford would be left to pick up what was left. Then, in a desperate attempt by the ACC to save itself, it added Cal and Stanford, leaving the last two to defend for themselves.

Because no Power 4 conference wants either Washington St or Oregon St, they're forced to merge with a lesser conference than where they were and, in the 2024 season, will be the only two left in what's left of the Pac.

Conference Cage

Teams stuck in conference with failed leadership

Not all teams in each conference want to be in their respective conferences. Every team in the ACC wants to be somewhere other than there. They don't like their media rights deal, which has caused lawsuits to be filed, turning the ACC into a mess.

The Pac-12 became so disorganized, and its members became so displeased with its leadership that the conference imploded. Because of contracts within the conference, teams are forced to stay within their conference, even if leadership fails them.

Proposed New Format Overview

Proposed New College Football Format

This proposed new format can potentially fix many current issues plaguing College Football.

Purposed Format

Fluid Conferences

Teams move up or down after the season.

Grouped Conferences

Grouped conferences for non-conference play.

Level of Play

Play teams of your same caliber.

Financial

Earn based on your performance on the field.

Conference Playoffs

All conferences have their own playoffs.

set up

Conference set up

All of the current football conferences would be dissolved, and six new conferences would be created. Teams placed within the conferences at the start of the format would be based on the final rankings of the previous year. This would allow every school to be placed in a conference so that every school would have a conference. It would also pull in independent schools, and the conferences would be ranked.

Scheduling

Scheduling of Conference and non-Conference games

Each team within a conference division will play each other during the season, providing nine games. They will also have to play one team from the opposite division within the conference and two non-conference games from the conference that you're grouped with. This will simplify team scheduling, and weak schedules will be eliminated.

Below is how the Premier and Elite Conferences schedules would be set up. All conferences would follow the same scheduling with their grouped conference.

Premier Conference

  • 9 in conference games, in the same division
  • 1 in conference game, from opposite division
  • 2 non-conference games with Elite Conference

Elite Conference

  • 9 in conference games, in the same division
  • 1 in conference game, from opposite division
  • 2 non-conference games with Premier Conference

Fluid

Fluid Conferences giving opportunity to all programs

At the end of the year, the bottom four teams of each conference will move to the conference below their current, and the top four will move up. This move will be reflected in next year's play.

Having fluid conferences provides a way for all schools to benefit based on their performance on the field. If you play well, you move up; if you don't, you move down. This style of fluid conferences will create massive viewership as the best teams play each other weekly. This will also eliminate weak schedules as teams play others that match their level.

This format will also prevent teams in lesser conferences from going undefeated, given a big bowl game, just to be crushed by a superior opponent. But, it will also allow teams not considered the blue-bloods of college football to move up as they play well on the field.

How it works

At the End of the Season

  1. Premier Conference

    At the end of the season, the bottom four will be moved to the Elite Conference for next years schedule.

  2. Elite Conference

    At the end of the season, the top four will be moved to the Premier Conference and the bottom four will be moved to the Champion Conference for next years schedule.

  3. Champion Conference

    At the end of the season, the top four will be moved to the Elite Conference and the bottom four will be moved to the Contender Conference for next years schedule.

  4. Contender Conference

    At the end of the season, the top four will be moved to the Champion Conference and the bottom four will be moved to the Competitor Conference for next years schedule.

  5. Competitor Conference

    At the end of the season, the top four will be moved to the Contender Conference and the bottom four will be moved to the Rookie Conference for next years schedule.

  6. Rookie Conference

    At the end of the season, the top four will be moved to the Competitor Conference for next years schedule.

Financial

Ran by networks, teams earn more than they should

These would be the default bare minimum payouts. Price tags could increase as networks bid among themselves for rights each year, leaving schools out of it. As schools move into conferences best suited for their performance, you play others to your caliber of play and are paid based on what you contribute to the sport. No more will schools use the success of other programs to reap rewards they don't deserve.

The new format could allow the larger TV network's priority when bidding for which conferences they'll have rights to. Current conference networks and other networks like TBS and TNT could be combined with streaming services like Peacock, Amazon Prime, Hulu, and more as secondary priorities. This would foster even more watchable content, with additional companies and networks jumping in and producing more money.

Championships

Championship Playoffs for each conference

Each conference would have an 8-team playoff for the conference championship at the end of the season. The Premier Conference Playoffs would be recognized as the national championship.

This format will create a larger-scale playoff system, six to be exact. Eight teams will battle it out for the championship and end the year in the top four to move up a conference.

Teams that don't make their conference playoffs could be matched with teams from their grouped conferences in regular bowl games. They, too, will be playing for a win in their bowl game and not to be placed in the bottom four of the conference by the end of the season.

Lawsuits

NCAA and Conference Lawsuits

Through the years, lawsuits have been filed with the NCAA and conferences due to issues arising from the current format. With the new format, it would all end, as every team would have the opportunity to move up and make more money.

How to

How to Move Up in conferences

Every team has the opportunity to move up and make more money. A program could achieve this by getting suitable coaching hires, recruiting at a high level, game planning, and in-game adjustments. A team's performance will allow them to end the season in the top four and move up to play better teams suited to their caliber.
